Our Solar System

Explore the planets and wonders of our solar system

The Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of our solar system. It is by far the most important source of energy for life on Earth.

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest and closest planet to the Sun. It has a thin atmosphere and experiences extreme temperatures.

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun. It is known for its thick, toxic atmosphere and extreme surface temperatures.

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet to support life, with vast oceans and diverse ecosystems.

Mars

Mars, the fourth planet from the Sun, is known for its red surface and is the most explored planet in the Solar System.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system. It has a famous storm called the Great Red Spot.

Saturn

Saturn is known for its extensive ring system made of ice and rock particles.

Uranus

Uranus is unique for its tilted axis, which makes it spin on its side compared to other planets.

Neptune

Neptune is the furthest planet from the Sun and is known for its deep blue color and intense storms.
